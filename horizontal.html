  • 2004

    Registers as an NGO in Kitale, and takes up 7 orphans.

  • 2006

    Laban travels to Germany for short term work and
    secures first significant financing of about US$20,000
    to purchase land and build a home;
    expands to about 30 kids.

  • 2008

    HJCH supports the first 2 kids to
    complete high school and begin college.

  • 2010

    One of the orphaned kids graduates top in high school
    and gets admission into Duke University in the USA;
    (HJCH hits 80 children mark)

  • 2011

    HJCH relocates into a one acre farm
    with semi-permanent structure to house kids.

  • 2014

    HJCH hits 150 mark in numbers of children served;
    more emphasis on education through mentorship
    given inspiring performance of Duke admittee

  • 2015

    HJCH hits 200 mark in numbers of children served.

  • 2018

    HJCH begins to look to establish
    a scholarship fund for the education of orphans
    as well as develop a sustainable income plan.
